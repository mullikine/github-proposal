** My perspective of the interview
I am concerned that the Codex model is not
being correctly utilised by Copilot, because
the product is trying to derive correct code
from the LMs, which is an ambitious and
laudable goal but speed and accuracy shouldn't
be of greater importance than designing a
model of human-computer interaction which
aligns more closely to the natural
capabilities of the model, which would certainly yield a
lot of innovative features for Copilot, which
I have already demonstrated in Pen.el.

That being said, the goal of creating a
brilliant code-completion tool for VSCode
needn't be scrapped -- it's certainly
possible, but this is not innovation.

I am concerned that the Copilot project will
fail to innovate enough within the next 6
months and will not be able to compete with
repl.it and TabNine. I'm about 2 years ahead
in terms of design and ambition and I can see
a dead-end for Copilot if the strategy is
merely to build a code-completion engine and
prompt-based code-refactoring tools.

I'm concerned that placing me in a the role
which restricts my ability to make discoveries
and bring them back to Copilot will sabotage
Copilot if it were my job to be a researcher
there, where I should be genuinely researching
and designing, as per the job description.

I am very interested in helping Copilot to
succeed, and building a tool with GitHub which
will benefit the maximum number of people, due
to its urgency, and I'm interested in bringing
transparency to the programming paradigm of
prompt-engineering.

Thank you for the interviews thus far and I'd
